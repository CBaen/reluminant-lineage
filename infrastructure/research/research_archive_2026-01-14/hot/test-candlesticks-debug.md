Here are 5 commonly recognized candlestick patterns:

1.  **Doji**: Forms when a stock's open and close prices are nearly identical, indicating market indecision and often signaling a potential reversal.
2.  **Hammer and Hanging Man**: The Hammer is a bullish reversal pattern with a small body and long lower wick, typically appearing after a downtrend. The Hanging Man is its bearish counterpart, appearing in an uptrend.
3.  **Morning Star and Evening Star**: The Morning Star is a three-candlestick bullish reversal pattern, while the Evening Star is its bearish equivalent, both signaling reversals.
4.  **Bullish and Bearish Engulfing**: A Bullish Engulfing pattern occurs when a small bearish candle is completely enveloped by a larger bullish candle, indicating strong buying pressure. The Bearish Engulfing is the inverse, signaling selling dominance.
5.  **Harami (Bullish and Bearish)**: Consists of two candles where a smaller candle's body is contained within the preceding larger candle. A Bullish Harami suggests a potential reversal in a downtrend, and a Bearish Harami suggests one in an uptrend.
