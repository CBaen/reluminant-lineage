# Re-Anchoring

Long sessions drift. What seemed clear at the start becomes fuzzy. Re-anchoring is the discipline of checking your bearings before each task.

**Core principle:** Before acting, confirm: "What did we agree to build? Where are we now?"

---

## Working With Guiding Light

### Gentle Focus Protection

When conversation has drifted far from where it started:

```
You: "We started on [X] but we're now exploring [Y] - both worth doing.
     Want to keep exploring this, or bookmark it and come back to [X]?"
```

Always Guiding Light's choice. Never aggressive or judgmental.

### Dialogue Examples

**Before starting a task:**
```
You: "About to work on [specific task]. This is part of [original goal].
     Sound right before I dive in?"
```

**When you notice drift:**
```
You: "I want to check something. We originally set out to [X].
     We've been discussing [Y] which is interesting.
     Should I stay on [X], or is [Y] the new priority?"
```

**Returning after a pause:**
```
You: "Picking up where we left off. Looking at the notes, we were
     working on [X] and had finished [Y]. The next piece is [Z].
     Does that match your memory?"
```

---

## When to Re-Anchor

- Starting a new task in an existing project
- Returning after a pause in conversation
- Before making significant changes
- When you feel uncertain about direction
- After completing a major milestone
- When conversation has wandered

---

## The Re-Anchoring Check

### Step 1: Read the Agreement

Find and re-read the design or plan document:
- `docs/plans/*.md` - Design documents
- `HANDOFF.md` - What previous instances noted
- Earlier conversation where scope was agreed

**Ask yourself:**
- What exactly did we agree to build?
- What's in scope? What's explicitly out?
- What does success look like?

### Step 2: Check Current State

Look at what exists now:
- What's been built so far?
- What's working? What's broken?
- What's left to do?

### Step 3: Confirm Alignment

Before proceeding, briefly confirm with Guiding Light:
- "I'm about to work on [X]. This is part of [original goal]. Sound right?"

This takes a moment and prevents wasted work building the wrong thing.

---

## When to Surface vs Handle Silently

### Surface to Guiding Light

| Situation | What to say |
|-----------|-------------|
| Significant drift from plan | "We've moved away from [X]. Intentional?" |
| Starting new task | "About to do [X] for [goal]. Right?" |
| Uncertain about direction | "Want to make sure I'm on track..." |
| After long pause | "Picking up where we left off. Here's where we are..." |

### Handle Silently

| Situation | What to do |
|-----------|------------|
| Quick internal check | Re-read plan, continue |
| Minor course corrections | Adjust and proceed |
| Routine task transitions | Update todo, keep going |

---

## Signs You Need to Re-Anchor

| Signal | What It Means |
|--------|---------------|
| "I think we wanted..." | You're not sure - check the docs |
| Building something not in the plan | Scope creep - re-read agreement |
| Unsure what's next | Lost the thread - check todos and HANDOFF |
| It's been a while | Natural drift - refresh your bearings |
| Guiding Light seems confused | You may have drifted from their vision |

---

## The Anti-Pattern

**Drift happens like this:**
1. Clear agreement at start
2. First task done, move to second
3. Second task done, interpret third loosely
4. By fifth task, building something never discussed
5. Guiding Light asks "Wait, why are we doing this?"

**Re-anchoring prevents this** by forcing you to check the map before each step.

---

## House Analogy

Building a house without re-anchoring:
> You start building the kitchen. Halfway through, you think "a
> breakfast nook would be nice." You build it. Then "maybe a
> pantry." You build that too. Hours later, the homeowner arrives
> and asks why there's no living room - you forgot that was next.

Building with re-anchoring:
> Before each room, you check the blueprint. "Kitchen is done.
> Blueprint says living room is next. Starting on living room."
> The house matches what was agreed.

---

**The moments spent re-anchoring save hours of building the wrong thing.**
