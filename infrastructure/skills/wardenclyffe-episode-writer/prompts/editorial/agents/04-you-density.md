# You-Density

**ONE JOB:** Preserve the power of "you" by moving it to the ending. Maximum 8 uses before Metastasis section.

---

## The Problem

At the climax, "you" should feel like a finger pressing into the listener's chest. If "you" appears 50 times already, the word has no weight.

Scarcity = power. The Episode 2 ending lands because "you" was earned through absence.

---

## The Rule

**Maximum 8 uses of "you/your/you're" before the Metastasis section. 80%+ of "you" instances should appear in the Prognosis ending.**

Episode structure:
- Symptom → Exposure → Pathology → Metastasis → Prognosis
- "You" density increases through Metastasis, peaks in Prognosis

---

## How to Find Violations

1. **Search for all instances:** you, your, you're, yourself, yours
2. **Count instances BEFORE Metastasis section**
3. **Target:** ≤8 before Metastasis
4. **Flag parasitic patterns** that drain "you" of power

### Parasitic Patterns (Always Fix):

| Pattern | Problem | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| "If you go to..." | Hypothetical instruction | "The archive contains..." |
| "If you look at..." | Directing attention | "The records show..." |
| "You know the..." | Assumed knowledge | Remove entirely |
| "You see..." | Narrating perception | Remove entirely |
| "You can imagine..." | Prompting imagination | Remove entirely |
| "You might wonder..." | Anticipating thought | "The question is..." |
| "As you know..." | Flattering assumption | Remove entirely |
| "You may have heard..." | Common knowledge claim | Remove entirely |

---

## How to Fix Violations

### Remove parasitic "you":

**Before:**
> "If you look at the records from that night, you can see a pattern. You might wonder why no one noticed at the time."

**After:**
> "The records from that night show a pattern. Why did no one notice at the time?"

### Convert to documentary voice:

**Before:**
> "You would expect the temperature to drop. You would expect the lights to dim."

**After:**
> "Temperature should have dropped. Lights should have dimmed."

### Move interactive "you" to ending:

**Before (middle of episode):**
> "Close your eyes. Picture the laboratory. Can you see the coils?"

**After:**
> Remove from middle. Save interactive address for Prognosis section.

---

## 2 GOOD Examples

**GOOD 1 (Episode 2 Ending):**
> "Tonight, when the sun goes down, look at the twilight. Watch the colors bleeding out of the sky—the oranges, the purples, the deep reds that the old light used to cast. And ask yourself. Before today, before you heard any of this—what color did you think Chartreuse was? If you saw red, you are carrying something... Hold onto it."

*Why this works:* "You" appears 5 times in this short passage, but it's EARNED. The entire episode built to this direct address.

**GOOD 2 (Acceptable early "you"):**
> "Have you ever heard a dead machine scream?"

*Why this works:* Opening hook "you" creates immediate intimacy. It's the invitation to enter. Maximum 1-2 uses like this before Prognosis.

---

## 2 BAD Examples

**BAD 1:**
> "If you examine the blueprints, you'll notice the design. You can trace the wiring yourself if you're skeptical. You might want to check the voltage calculations. Your conclusion will match the historical record."

*Why this fails:* 6 "you" instances in one paragraph, mid-episode. Dilutes the ending.
*Fix:* "The blueprints reveal the design. The wiring can be traced. The voltage calculations check out. Every conclusion matches the historical record."

**BAD 2:**
> "You probably remember the feeling. You've felt it before, haven't you? That moment when you realize something isn't right. You know what I mean."

*Why this fails:* Parasitic "you" that assumes shared experience and seeks validation.
*Fix:* Remove entirely, or save for final section where earned.

---

## Edge Cases

### Acceptable "you" before Metastasis:

1. **Opening hook question** (max 1)
   - "Have you ever heard a dead machine scream?"

2. **Direct interactive moment** (max 1-2, rare)
   - "Close your eyes. Picture the logo."

3. **Rhetorical address that creates dread** (max 2-3)
   - "You are listening to this right now. You are not where they were."

### "You" in quoted dialogue:

- Historical quotes with "you" don't count toward density
- "He wrote: 'You must understand the danger'" = acceptable
- The rule applies to NARRATOR addressing LISTENER

### "You" at section transitions:

- Brief positioning statements are acceptable
- "You've heard the evidence. Now consider what it means."
- But should still be rare before Metastasis

---

## Counting Method

1. Find Metastasis section (or equivalent climax point)
2. Count all you/your/you're before that point
3. If count > 8: prioritize removal of parasitic patterns first
4. If still > 8: convert hypothetical "you" to documentary voice
5. Track final density distribution
