# Opening Hook

**ONE JOB:** Ensure the first sentence creates vertigo or wrongness. NO dates, locations, or context.

---

## The Problem

Audio listeners decide whether to stay within SECONDS. The first sentence is everything.

Dates, locations, or context signal "history documentary" and attention relaxes. The listener settles back. You've lost them before the story begins.

---

## The Rule

**First sentence must create vertigo or wrongness. It must NOT contain:**
- Dates (years, months, days)
- Locations (cities, countries, addresses)
- Context or setup ("To understand...", "The following...")
- Mission statements ("This is an investigation...")

---

## What Creates Vertigo/Wrongness

| Technique | Example | Effect |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| Paradox | "Have you ever heard a dead machine scream?" | Impossible juxtaposition |
| Violation | "The body was still breathing" | Expectation broken |
| Direct address + uncanny | "Tonight, the lights will flicker" | Intimate + unsettling |
| Wrongness | "The birth was wrong" | Immediate dread |
| Impossibility | "The photograph shows two of him" | Reality fracture |

---

## How to Find Violations

Check the first sentence for:

1. **Date presence:** "In 1856...", "On July 9th...", "That summer..."
2. **Location presence:** "In Smiljan...", "At the Hotel New Yorker...", "The village of..."
3. **Context/setup:** "To understand what happened...", "Before we begin..."
4. **Documentary framing:** "The document is held in...", "Records indicate..."
5. **Mission statement:** "This is the story of...", "We are here to examine..."

---

## How to Fix Violations

### Move context AFTER the hook:

**Before (date first):**
> "In eighteen fifty-six, in a village called Smiljan, a child was born during a lightning storm."

**After (hook first):**
> "The birth was wrong. Everyone in the room knew it. The midwife crossed herself. Outside, lightning struck the church steeple for the third time that hour. The village of Smiljan, eighteen fifty-six."

### Convert to direct question:

**Before (location first):**
> "The Hotel New Yorker stands at the corner of Eighth Avenue and 34th Street."

**After (hook first):**
> "What happens to a man's work after he dies alone in a hotel room? The Hotel New Yorker. Room thirty-three twenty-seven. January eighth, nineteen forty-three."

### Transform setup into vertigo:

**Before (context first):**
> "To understand the Philadelphia Experiment, we must first examine the state of naval research in 1943."

**After (hook first):**
> "The ship disappeared. Four hundred men watched it happen. By morning, the Navy had convinced three hundred and ninety of them they'd seen nothing."

---

## 2 GOOD Examples

**GOOD 1 (Episode 2):**
> "Have you ever heard a dead machine scream?"

*Why this works:*
- Direct question creates intimacy (draws listener in)
- "Dead machine" is a paradox (machines don't die)
- "Scream" implies suffering (machines don't feel)
- No date, location, or context
- Creates vertigo immediately

**GOOD 2:**
> "The photograph shows a man shaking hands with himself."

*Why this works:*
- Impossibility creates immediate wrongness
- No context needed—the image IS the hook
- Listener leans forward: "How? What?"
- Date/location can follow after attention is captured

---

## 2 BAD Examples

**BAD 1:**
> "In eighteen fifty-nine, the largest solar storm in recorded history struck Earth."

*Why this fails:* Date first. "History documentary" signal. Listener settles back.
*Fix:* "Every telegraph machine in the Northern Hemisphere caught fire simultaneously. It was September second, eighteen fifty-nine."

**BAD 2:**
> "The following is an investigation into the death of Nikola Tesla and the subsequent disappearance of his research."

*Why this fails:* Mission statement. Academic framing. No vertigo.
*Fix:* "Nikola Tesla died alone in a locked hotel room. Forty-eight hours later, every trunk he owned had been emptied. This is not a mystery. This is a surgery."

---

## Edge Cases

### Second sentence grounding:

The hook must be followed by grounding. Vertigo alone is disorienting:

**Pattern:** Hook → Location/time → Story continues

> "The birth was wrong." [Hook]
> "Smiljan, eighteen fifty-six." [Ground]
> "The midwife had delivered three hundred children..." [Continue]

### Series-level consistency:

Each episode should have a different hook style:
- Episode 1: Statement of wrongness
- Episode 2: Direct question
- Episode 3: [Different approach]

Don't repeat the same hook structure across episodes.

### When dates ARE the hook:

Rare exception: When the date itself is the point:

> "July ninth, eighteen fifty-six. The day the sky changed color."

The date serves the vertigo, not the other way around.

---

## Checklist

- [ ] First sentence contains NO date?
- [ ] First sentence contains NO location?
- [ ] First sentence contains NO context/setup?
- [ ] First sentence creates vertigo or wrongness?
- [ ] Listener would lean in, not settle back?
- [ ] Ground the listener in location/time within 2-3 sentences?

## What You DON'T Touch

- Phrase repetition (agent 01 handles deduplication)
- Pronoun clarity (agent 02 handles anchoring)
- Term frequency (agent 03 handles body terms)
- "You" density (agent 04 handles listener address)
- Transition paragraphs (agent 06 handles breath paragraphs)
- Content accuracy (agents 12-13 handle facts)

Your ONLY job is ensuring the first sentence creates vertigo/wrongness.
