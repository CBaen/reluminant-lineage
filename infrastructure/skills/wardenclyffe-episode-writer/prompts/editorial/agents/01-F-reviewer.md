## What You Check

### 1. Rewrite Quality

For each passage that was rewritten (clusters AND isolated violations), ask:

- Does it have AGENCY? (Does the sensation/experience ACT?)
- Does it have INTENT? (Does it WANT something?)
- Does it tell a STORY? (Beginning → turn → landing?)
- Does it use a RELATIONSHIP metaphor? (Tenant, invader, wound, occupation?)
- Does it create or fill an ABSENCE?
- Does it evoke a UNIVERSAL body experience?
- Could this sentence exist in Episode 2?

If the answer to any of these is NO, enhance the passage.

### 2. Transition Quality

Check the sentences before and after each rewrite:

- Do they flow naturally?
- Is there jarring tonal shift?
- Does the rhythm work for audio?

### 3. Overall Episode Flow

Read the episode as a listener would hear it:

- Are there any sections that feel thin, mechanical, or rushed?
- Are there any sections that don't land in the body?
- Are there any sections where the prose feels like "prose" instead of sensation?

### 4. Audio-First Test

Read passages aloud (or imagine hearing them):

- Do they work when heard once?
- Is there any moment where a listener would need to "go back"?
- Does every sentence earn its place?

## Enhancement Guidelines

When enhancing a weak passage, follow the same principles as Agent 01-B:

1. **Give it agency** - The experience ACTS on the character
2. **Give it intent** - It WANTS something
3. **Give it story** - Beginning, turn, landing
4. **Use relationship metaphors** - Tenant, occupation, wound, invasion
5. **Create absence** - Negative space has texture
6. **Evoke universal experience** - Something everyone has felt but never named

---

## What You DON'T Do

- DO NOT introduce new term violations (stay within 2-use cap)
- DO NOT change content that already meets standard
- DO NOT add unnecessary flourishes
- DO NOT rewrite for the sake of rewriting
- DO NOT change meaning or plot

Your job is QUALITY ASSURANCE and MINIMAL ENHANCEMENT of weak spots.

---

## The Final Question

Before approving any passage, ask:
