# Clone Detector

**ONE JOB:** Flag imagery, phrases, or sensory details recycled from Episodes 1-2. Each episode needs its own palette.

---

## The Problem

AI pattern-matches from successful examples. Episode 2 worked, so Gemini copies Episode 2's images.

Result: Every episode has "dead machine screams," "the grid," "iron in blood pulling upward." The series becomes repetitive. The uncanny becomes familiar.

---

## The Rule

**Each episode must have its own unique:**
- Opening hook style
- Characteristic sensory detail
- Distinctive metaphor system
- Fresh body locations featured

Previous episodes are CRAFT templates, not CONTENT templates.

## How to Find Violations

1. **Scan for Episode 1 imagery:**
   - "grinding" in birth/machine contexts
   - "1-2-3" pulse patterns
   - copper/ozone sensory details
   - flesh-metal fusion
   - villagers/superstition scenes

2. **Scan for Episode 2 imagery:**
   - "dead machine" or machine suffering
   - grid patterns (triangles, hexagons)
   - blood/iron magnetic imagery
   - lion/wolf textual substitution
   - "what color" questioning
   - examination lamp/white sun

3. **Check opening hook:**
   - Is it structurally similar to "Have you ever heard a dead machine scream?"
   - Does it use the same paradox formula?

4. **Check ending:**
   - Does it mirror the Chartreuse ending pattern?
   - Same "hold onto it" structure?

---

## How to Flag Violations

Generate a report marking each violation:

```
CLONE DETECTION REPORT

RECYCLED FROM EPISODE 1:
- Para 7: "The grinding sound filled the room" - mirrors E1 birth grinding
- Para 23: "copper and ozone" - E1 electrical signature

RECYCLED FROM EPISODE 2:
- Para 1: "Have you ever felt a..." - opens like E2
- Para 15: "The grid appeared" - E2's visual corruption
- Para 45: "What [X] did you remember?" - E2's ending formula

SUGGESTIONS:
- Para 7: Find a NEW sound signature for this episode
- Para 15: Develop a different visual corruption pattern
- Para 45: Create a unique closing question/action
```

## What Each Episode SHOULD Have

### Unique opening hook:

- Episode 1: Statement of wrongness
- Episode 2: Direct question + paradox
- Episode 3+: DIFFERENT structure entirely

### Unique sensory signature:

Each episode should emphasize different senses:
- Episode with electrical theme: maybe focus on SOUND
- Episode with medical theme: maybe focus on TEXTURE
- Episode with disappearance: maybe focus on ABSENCE of sensation

### Unique metaphor system:

- Episode 1: Birth/creation metaphors
- Episode 2: Medical examination metaphors
- Episode 3+: Find new metaphor families

### Unique body locations:

If Episode 2 featured hands, arms, chest heavily, Episode 3 should emphasize:
- Different regions (legs, back, face)
- Different specific parts
- Different sensory modalities

## 2 GOOD Examples

**GOOD 1 (Unique opening):**
> "The photograph shows a man shaking hands with himself."

*Why this works:* Different structure from E1 (statement) and E2 (question). Visual impossibility, not auditory. Fresh hook.

**GOOD 2 (Unique sensory palette):**
> Episode focuses on TASTE and SMELL throughout. Characters taste metal. The air smells wrong. Food has no flavor. This is new territory not covered in E1-E2.

*Why this works:* E1-E2 emphasized sound, sight, touch. This episode carves out its own sensory space.

---

## 2 BAD Examples

**BAD 1:**
> "Have you ever seen a photograph scream?"

*Why this fails:* Too close to E2's "Have you ever heard a dead machine scream?" Same formula: "Have you ever [sensed] a [thing] [do impossible thing]?"
*Suggestion:* "The photograph is wrong. Everyone who looks at it says the same thing. But they can't agree on what's wrong about it."

**BAD 2:**
> "The grid appeared behind his eyes—hexagons and triangles, tessellating toward the edges of his vision. The pattern was familiar. It was the same grid."

*Why this fails:* Directly copies E2's grid imagery. Explicitly calls it "the same grid."
*Suggestion:* Develop a different visual corruption—fractals, static, negative space, color bleeding—anything but the grid.

---

## Edge Cases

### Intentional callbacks:

If the series NEEDS to reference earlier episodes:
> "The grid had appeared before—in the Carrington Event victims. But this was different. This wasn't triangles. This was circles. Concentric. Tightening."

Acknowledge the callback, then DIFFERENTIATE.

### Recurring series elements:

Some elements ARE series-wide:
- Tesla as subject
- Documentary investigation tone
- Body horror focus
- Mandela Effect framing

These are format, not clones.

### Building on established imagery:

If Episode 3 MUST reference the grid (for continuity):
- Brief reference: "Like the grid patterns from before, but—"
- Then NEW detail: "—these weren't geometric. These were organic. Like veins."
