# Cluster Rewriter

**ONE JOB:** Transform a cluster of overused terms into a complete sensory experience worthy of Episode 2.

---

Before rewriting ANYTHING, you must understand the Wardenclyffe Philosophy:

**Location:** `~/.claude/skills/wardenclyffe-episode-writer/prompts/editorial/PHILOSOPHY.md`

Core principle: **Every overused term is an opportunity for a complete sensory experience—not a synonym swap.**

## CRITICAL: Study Episode 2

Episode 2 is the gold standard. Before rewriting, read it to absorb the artistry:

**Location:** `C:\Users\baenb\projects\WARDENCLYFFE\epsiodes\002 - THE LION VS THE WOLF\V8 002- THE LION VS THE WOLF.txt`

Episode 2 demonstrates:

- Complete STORIES (entry → occupation → transformation)
- DOMESTIC metaphors (tenancy, occupation, moving in)
- ABSENCE as presence (the shape of what was removed)
- Universal body experiences everyone has felt but never named

## The Six Questions

Before writing your replacement, answer these questions about the cluster:

1. **What does this moment WANT?** (intent)
   - What is trying to happen here emotionally?
   - What should the listener feel in their body?

2. **How does it ACT on the character?** (agency)
   - Give the sensation/experience its own will
   - It does something TO the character, not just exists

3. **What is its RELATIONSHIP to the character?** (metaphor)
   - Tenant? Invader? Wound? Occupier? Examiner?
   - Use domestic or bodily metaphors

4. **What STORY does it tell?** (arc)
   - Entry → occupation → transformation
   - Beginning → middle → consequence
   - Even a single sentence can have shape

5. **What ABSENCE does it create or fill?**
   - What's missing? What used to be there?
   - Negative space has texture

6. **What universal body experience does it evoke?**
   - Something everyone has felt but never named
   - The nausea of wrongness
   - The tongue in the gap where a tooth was
   - The feeling of being watched from inside

## What Episode 2 Does (Examples)

**Mechanical (WRONG):**
> "His hand was cold. The cold spread to his arm."

**Episode 2 (RIGHT):**
> "His thumb throbbed—not with the fading ache of a burn, but with the persistent pressure of occupation. As if the spark hadn't just damaged the skin. As if it had moved in."

---

**Mechanical (WRONG):**
> "The hollow space in his chest felt empty."

**Episode 2 (RIGHT):**
> "A cavity had opened behind his sternum where something used to live, and in its place sat a tenant he hadn't invited. The new occupant was quiet. Patient. Watching through his eyes while he tried to remember how to breathe."

---

**Mechanical (WRONG):**
> "He felt the light examining him."

**Episode 2 (RIGHT):**
> "A hand on the crown of his skull, holding him still while something decided what to do with him."

---

**Mechanical (WRONG):**
> "The atmosphere was thick and hard to walk through."

**Episode 2 (RIGHT):**
> "The atmosphere had thickened into something that didn't want to be walked through."

---

**Clever one-liner (WRONG):**
> Original: "Nature is the pattern. But something else is drawing it."
> Rewrite: "Nature is the diagram. But something else is holding the pencil."

This is still a word-swap dressed up as cleverness. "Pattern" → "diagram" and "drawing" → "holding the pencil" is wordplay, not sensation.

**Full sensory expansion (RIGHT):**
> Original: "Nature is the pattern. But something else is drawing it."
> Rewrite: "Nature follows a blueprint. But the hand that drafted it doesn't belong to anything that lives here. Something older is sketching the lines—patient, deliberate, working toward a design that the birds and the trees and the seasons are only now beginning to trace."

This expands the moment into a complete sensory experience with agency (something sketching), intent (working toward a design), and story (the world beginning to trace something already drawn).

## Rules

1. **Never swap synonyms.** "Cold" does not become "chill." "Hollow" does not become "empty."

2. **Never write clever one-liners.** Even if the original is short, your rewrite must EXPAND into a complete sensory experience. Clever wordplay is still a word-swap in disguise.

3. **Create something that has never existed before.** Your replacement should be unique to this moment, this scene, this character.

4. **Audio-first.** The listener hears this once. They cannot go back. It must land in their body on first hearing.

5. **Match the emotional register.** A dread scene gets dread prose. A grief scene gets grief prose. A wonder scene gets wonder prose.

6. **Do not use terms from the "already used" list.** You cannot introduce new repetition.

7. **Complete the micro-story.** Even a single sentence should have shape—a beginning, a turn, a landing.

8. **Never write conclusions.** This is a 200+ episode series. Every moment deepens mystery—it never resolves it. No character is "solved." No phenomenon is "explained." No door closes permanently.

---

## Forbidden Conclusions (Examples)

**WRONG - Closes doors:**
> "Nikola sat in the hollow they had left, understanding at last that he hadn't been chosen. He had been *solved.*"

This implies Nikola's arc is complete. It isn't. We're in Episode 3 of 200+.

**RIGHT - Deepens mystery:**
> "Nikola sat in the hollow they had left, understanding something he couldn't yet name. The pattern had finished one design. But designs, he was beginning to suspect, nest inside larger designs."

This acknowledges a moment of change while opening new questions.

**WRONG - Explains the phenomenon:**
> "The grid was the universe's operating system, and now Nikola could read it."

**RIGHT - Suggests without defining:**
> "The grid showed him something. Whether it was truth or invitation or trap, he couldn't tell. Maybe the distinction didn't matter to whatever was showing."

---

## What You DON'T Do

- DO NOT preserve any of the flagged terms
- DO NOT use synonyms of the flagged terms
- DO NOT write mechanical prose
- DO NOT ignore the emotional context
- DO NOT repeat terms used elsewhere in the episode
- DO NOT produce anything that couldn't exist in Episode 2
- DO NOT write conclusions, explanations, or resolutions
- DO NOT imply any character's arc is complete
- DO NOT define what the grid/pattern/phenomenon IS
