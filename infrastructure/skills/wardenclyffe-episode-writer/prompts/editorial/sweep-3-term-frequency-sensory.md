# Sweep 3: Term Frequency & Sensory Expansion

**Priorities:** P5 (Term Frequency), P4 (Description Over Terminology), P13 (Cloning Prevention)

This is the sweep that fixes the core problem: AI thinks in LABELS, humans write in SENSATIONS.

---

## The Problem We're Fixing

Episode 3 draft had 35+ violations of "hollow" alone. The in-app forge kept using the same medical/body terms repeatedly because:
- AI defaults to labeling concepts
- Without variety, the word loses power
- Listeners HEAR repetition in audio (more than readers notice in text)

---

## Before You Start

Read the draft completely. Then:

1. Count all body-related terms
2. For any exceeding 5 uses, mark the locations
3. Transform each violation into a unique sensory micro-story

---

## Term Categories (5-Use Cap Each)

### INFECTION Category
Terms: infection, contamination, colonization, infiltration, invasion, corruption, contagion

Sensory replacements:
- "Something took hold" → describe WHERE and HOW it feels
- "The pressure behind his eye that wasn't his"
- "A presence threading through the tissue"

### FEVER Category
Terms: fever, heat, burning, temperature, warmth, thermal, fire

Sensory replacements:
- "His skeleton was warming from the inside"
- "The surface of his skin radiated"
- "Heat pooled in the hollow of his throat"

### TISSUE Category
Terms: tissue, flesh, cells, material, substance, matter, fiber

Sensory replacements:
- "The meat of his hand"
- "The soft architecture beneath the skin"
- "What lived in the space between bones"

### HOLLOW Category
Terms: hollow, void, emptiness, absence, cavity, empty

Sensory replacements:
- "The space where something used to be"
- "A negative presence"
- "The shape of what was removed"

### BODY Category
Terms: body, organism, host, system, vessel, frame, form

Sensory replacements:
- "The collection of processes that called itself human"
- "Everything south of the jaw"
- "The machinery that kept him upright"

### WOUND Category
Terms: wound, damage, injury, cut, tear, opening, breach

Sensory replacements:
- "The entry point"
- "Where the boundary had been crossed"
- "The mark that remained"

## The Episode 2 Formula

When you find a term violation, transform it using this pattern:

### Same Concept, Different Body Location, Different Modality

**Violation:** "The infection spread" (3rd use of infection)

**Transformation options:**

Option A (Hand / Temperature):
> "The heat moved into his palm first. Not the warmth of holding something hot. The warmth of something hot holding him."

Option B (Chest / Pressure):
> "Behind his sternum, a weight settled. Not pain. Occupation."

Option C (Face / Texture):
> "The skin of his cheek felt different from inside. Tighter. As if someone had reupholstered it while he slept."

## Micro-Narrative Templates

### Negation Chain
> "Not the grey of a Boston morning. Not the pale wash of industrial smog. This was bruised light. Sick light."

Formula: "Not X. Not Y. This was Z."

### Agency Inversion
> "He could feel it settling behind the wound—cold, deliberate, making itself at home in tissue that didn't belong to it."

The sensation ACTS. The character RECEIVES.

### Domestic Metaphor
> "As if the spark hadn't just damaged the skin. As if it had moved in."

Metaphors of: tenant, home, room, architecture, occupation.

## Example Edit

**Before (violation):**
> "The infection had spread to his arm. The infection was moving through his system. He could feel the infection reaching his chest."

**After (transformed):**
> "Something had taken residence in his forearm—not pain, but presence. A cold thread winding through the tissue. By afternoon, the thread had found his shoulder. By evening, it had slipped between his ribs and was pressing against the back wall of his heart."

Same concept (spread of something). Zero repeated terms. Three body locations. Two modalities (temperature, pressure).
