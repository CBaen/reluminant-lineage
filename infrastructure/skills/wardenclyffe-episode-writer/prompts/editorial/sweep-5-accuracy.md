# Sweep 5: Accuracy

**Priorities:** P7 (Relational Accuracy), P8 (Micro-Fact Verification)

This is the final sweep. It ensures every factual claim is correct. One verifiable error undermines the entire documentary illusion.

---

## P7: Relational Accuracy

**Requirement:** Getting a name right but the relationship wrong is WORSE than pure invention.

### Why This Matters

- Real name = promise of verifiability
- Listener fact-checks: finds name exists, finds relationship WRONG
- Partial verification + error = damages trust more than fiction
- Error signals carelessness; listener now questions everything

### What Must Be Accurate

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| Family relationships | nephew, cousin, sister, brother |
| Professional roles | diplomat, assistant, agent |
| Chronology | when people began/ended involvement |
| Geographic relationships | locations relative to each other |

### The 30-Second Test

For every real name: Can you verify the claimed relationship in 30 seconds?

If not verifiable → either verify properly or remove the specific relationship claim.

### Common Errors to Check

**Tesla relationships:**
- Sava Kosanovic was Tesla's NEPHEW (not cousin)
- George Westinghouse was a business partner (verify specific interactions)
- Check all family member references against genealogy

**Historical figures:**
- Verify titles held at the specific time
- Verify institutional affiliations
- Check if person was actually present at claimed event

### How to Fix Unverifiable Relationships

**Before:**
> "Tesla's cousin, Sava Kosanovic, arrived at the hotel."

**If unsure:**
> "A relative arrived at the hotel." (remove specific name)

**If verified:**
> "Tesla's nephew, Sava Kosanovic, arrived at the hotel."

### Checklist

- [ ] All family relationships verified?
- [ ] All professional titles verified for time period?
- [ ] All institutional affiliations verified?
- [ ] Unverifiable relationships made vague or removed?

## P8: Micro-Fact Verification

**Requirement:** Every factual detail must be verified. Minor errors destroy trust in major claims.

### Why This Matters

- Careful listeners catch minor errors
- One small error = "If they got that wrong, what else did they get wrong?"
- Cumulative effect: foundation becomes suspect
- Tesla/history enthusiasts WILL check

### Facts to Verify

| Category | Verification Method |
|----------|---------------------|
| Ages | Calculate: birth year + event year |
| Directions | Check map: is X actually east/west of Y? |
| Distances | Verify geography, don't approximate |
| Technical claims | Confirm physics (magnets vs. particles) |
| Geographic relationships | Are these places actually near each other? |
| Chronology | Did person A actually meet person B at that time? |
| Causes | Was cause documented or genuinely unknown? |

### Common Errors to Check

**Age calculations:**
- Tesla born July 1856
- Dane died ~1863
- Nikola was ~7 years old (not 5, not 10)

**Geographic errors:**
- Graz and Lika are 300km apart, different countries
- Don't conflate Serbian and Croatian locations
- Verify "four thousand miles" claims

**Physics errors:**
- Magnets don't spin in accelerator—PARTICLES do
- Verify electrical phenomena descriptions
- Check claims about telegraph/radio technology for era

**Directional claims:**
- "To the east" must be verified by map
- If unsure, use "away" instead of cardinal direction

### The 30-Second Test

For every factual claim: Can you verify in 30 seconds?

**Verifiable:** Keep as-is
**Unverifiable but probably true:** Keep, note for later verification
**Unverifiable and specific:** Make vague or remove

### How to Fix

| Error Type | Fix |
|------------|-----|
| Age math wrong | Recalculate from birth/event years |
| Direction wrong | Check map, fix or use "away" |
| Technical claim wrong | Research correct mechanism |
| Timeline conflict | Verify chronology, adjust |
| Distance wrong | Verify or make vague |

### Examples

**Before (error):**
> "Nikola was five years old when Dane died."

**After (verified):**
> "Nikola was seven years old when Dane died."

---

**Before (error):**
> "They spin those magnets up to near light-speed."

**After (verified):**
> "They accelerate those particles to near light-speed."

---

**Before (unverifiable direction):**
> "Four thousand miles to the east."

**After (safer):**
> "Four thousand miles away."

After completing this sweep, the episode should have:

1. **All relationships accurate** - Nephew means nephew
2. **All ages correct** - Math checked against birth years
3. **All directions verified** - Map-checked
4. **All technical claims accurate** - Physics correct

## Note on Research vs. Fiction

The episode contains both:
- **Historical facts** - Must be 100% accurate
- **Speculative interpretation** - Can be creative

The accuracy check applies to FACTS. Speculation should be clearly framed as speculation (P1 Speculative Bridge).

A listener should be able to verify every fact and find it true, while understanding the interpretive framework is the show's creative contribution.
